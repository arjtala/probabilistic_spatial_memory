\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Egocentric video understanding and episodic memory.}
Ego4D~\cite{grauman2022ego4d} established look-back QA at
3{,}000-hour scale; EgoSchema~\cite{mangalam2023egoschema} exposed
the temporal-grounding collapse that motivates our prefilter pipeline.
The literal ``where did I leave my keys?'' task was posed by
Barmann et al.~\cite{barmann2022keys}; Di and Xie~\cite{di2024grounded}
unified grounded QA over long egocentric video, and Chen et
al.~\cite{chen2025groundedmultihop} extended it to multi-hop chains.
Plizzari et al.~\cite{plizzari2025spatial} show spatial cognition is
critical for object localization across time scales,
EgoMask~\cite{liang2025egomask} benchmarks fine-grained
spatiotemporal grounding in first-person video, and
ESOM~\cite{manigrasso2026esom} maintains a streaming object memory
whose 4\% online success rate underscores the hardness of egocentric
streaming retrieval. \psm{} is structurally orthogonal: it provides a
bounded-memory spatial-temporal substrate that any MLLM grounding
method can rerank, without retraining a new grounding head---an
instance of the VideoRAG pattern~\cite{jeong2025videorag}.

\paragraph{Memory-augmented video LLMs.}
MovieChat~\cite{song2024moviechat}, MA-LMM~\cite{he2024malmm},
Flash-VStream~\cite{zhang2024flashvstream}, and
LifelongMemory~\cite{wang2024lifelongmemory} tackle bounded-memory
long-video understanding; more recently,
HERMES~\cite{zhang2026hermes} arranges the KV cache as hierarchical
memory, InfiniPot-V~\cite{kim2025infinipotv} imposes a training-free
hard cap with 94\% reduction, and SAVEMem~\cite{wu2025savemem}
adds 3-tier semantic-aware retention.
All bound memory in \emph{token} space---dense-to-sparse merging,
KV-cache compression, or recurrent state---whereas \psm{} bounds
memory in \emph{geographic} space (H3 cells) and runs upstream of
any MLLM, returning a top-$K$ candidate set the downstream model
reranks without retraining.
Direct comparison is a category error: these methods compress what
the MLLM \emph{sees}; \psm{} determines \emph{which frames} the
MLLM is shown. The two are complementary: \psm{} as front-end
retrieval, token-space methods as MLLM-internal compression.

\paragraph{Streaming search and spatial indexing.}
DiskANN~\cite{jayaram2019diskann}, SPANN~\cite{chen2021spann}, and
locally-adaptive vector quantization~\cite{aguerrebere2024lvq}
deliver billion-point ANN with bounded per-query cost, but target
near-static corpora with no spatial constraint.
\psm{} adds two capabilities: queries optionally restrict to an H3
$k$-ring neighborhood---the ``near here'' axis ANN does not
expose---and ingestion is fully streaming, with bounded per-cell state
aged out by the ring buffer.
The H3 hex grid~\cite{brodsky2018h3} replaces R-tree-style
data-driven subdivisions~\cite{guttman1984rtrees} with equidistant
neighbors and globally-stable cell ids; the probabilistic primitives
(HyperLogLog~\cite{flajolet2007hyperloglog}, reservoir
sampling~\cite{vitter1985reservoir}) are standard---the contribution
is their composition under the \caponek{} query knob.
