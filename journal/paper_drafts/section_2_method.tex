\section{Probabilistic Spatial Memory}
\label{sec:method}

\psm{} is a streaming spatial-temporal index that ingests
$(t, \mathrm{lat}, \mathrm{lng}, \mathbf{e})$ tuples---timestamp,
geographic position, and a $d$-dimensional CLIP embedding---and
supports bounded-memory top-$K$ similarity search at any time.
Three layered probabilistic data structures enforce the memory bound.

\subsection{H3 spatial tessellation}
\label{sec:method-h3}

We tessellate the wearer's trajectory with
H3~\cite{brodsky2018h3}, a hexagonal hierarchical geospatial index.
Each tuple maps to its containing H3 cell at resolution~$r$
(default~12, ${\sim}9$\,m hex edges, room scale; we sweep
$r \in \{10, 11, 12, 13\}$ in \S\ref{sec:results-main}, covering
street to sub-room granularity). H3 cells are global identifiers:
cell IDs are comparable across recordings, sessions, and devices.

\subsection{Time-decayed ring buffer of HyperLogLog sketches}
\label{sec:method-ring-buffer}

Each H3 cell maintains a ring buffer of $C$ HyperLogLog
sketches~\cite{flajolet2007hyperloglog} that rotate on a
configurable time-window cadence (default 30\,s). Ingesting a
tuple hashes the frame's embedding into the current sketch;
when the window elapses the pointer advances and old sketches
expire as the ring wraps, enforcing strict bounded-time retention.
The sketches serve two roles: they provide cardinality estimates
of distinct events per cell and they expose the cell's
\emph{temporal envelope}
$(t_{\min}, t_{\max})$, which \psm{} returns as the bucket window
that the downstream MLLM consumes as its time-range hint.

\paragraph{Memory bound.} Each HLL sketch occupies $2^p$ bytes at
precision $p$ (default $p{=}14 \Rightarrow$ 16\,KiB); a cell's
ring buffer costs $C \cdot 16\,\mathrm{KiB} = 960\,\mathrm{KiB}$
at $C{=}60$, independent of recording duration.

\subsection{Reservoir-sampled exemplars}
\label{sec:method-exemplars}

HLL sketches yield counts and time envelopes but not the
embeddings needed for cosine scoring. We store embeddings via
\emph{Algorithm~R reservoir sampling}~\cite{vitter1985reservoir}
per H3 cell (capacity $R{=}128$): after ingesting $n > R$ frames
into a cell, the reservoir holds a uniformly random $R$-sized
subset.
At $R = 128$ and CLIP-L's $d = 768$, each cell occupies
$128 \cdot 768 \cdot 4 \approx 384$\,KiB; 100 cells store
${\sim}38$\,MB vs.\ ${\sim}200$\,MB for a flat embedding bank
over the same recording. The gap widens with duration:
brute-force grows $\Theta(N)$; \psm{} grows
$\Theta(R \cdot |\mathrm{cells}|)$, bounded by the physical area
covered, not recording length.

\paragraph{TurboQuant codec.} An optional 2-/3-/4-bit codec
(TurboQuant-style streaming vector
quantization~\cite{aguerrebere2024lvq}) compresses stored
exemplars 5--10$\times$ at no Hit@5 cost vs.\ \texttt{raw}
float32. All main results use \texttt{raw}; the codec targets
on-device deployment.

\subsection{Top-$K$ query with \caponek}
\label{sec:method-query}

A query takes a vector $\mathbf{q}$ and an optional
$(\mathrm{lat}, \mathrm{lng})$ center with k-ring radius.
Aggregation proceeds in two phases:
(i)~for each H3 cell in scope, return the top-$\min(\caponek,\;
\texttt{cell.reservoir.size})$ exemplars by
$\cos(\mathbf{q}, \mathbf{e}_i)$;
(ii)~sort the union by similarity and emit the top-$K$
with each cell's ring-buffer-derived bucket window.
Setting $\caponek{=}1$ enforces strict spatial diversity (one
slot per cell); $\caponek{=}K$ reduces to plain top-$K$ over
all reservoir exemplars; intermediate values trade off smoothly
(\S\ref{sec:results-main}).
Per-partition result limits are standard in database query planning;
the novelty here is the composition of this knob with H3 spatial
tessellation, time-decayed HLL sketches, and reservoir sampling under
streaming constraints.
Ingest is $O(1)$ amortized---H3 lookup, HLL hash, reservoir
update---at ${\sim}3\,\mu$s per frame; queries run at any time
after the first ingest (\S\ref{sec:results-memory-latency}).
