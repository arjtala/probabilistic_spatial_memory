\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A wearable AI assistant sees the world through the user's eyes for hours
per day. To answer look-back questions---``where did I leave my keys?'',
``what was that street sign?''---it must retrieve relevant moments from
its own past. The straightforward approach embeds every frame with a
vision--language encoder (CLIP, SigLIP, EVA) and runs cosine-similarity
retrieval over the resulting bank. This bank, however, grows linearly in
recording duration: at 1\,fps with CLIP-L 768-dim float32 vectors, one
hour costs ${\sim}10$\,MB and a full day ${\sim}200$\,MB; on-device
limits bind in days. Although system-architecture work on always-on
wearable AI~\cite{lee2025aria2} identifies a persistent
spatial-temporal index as a first-class component, no bounded-memory
implementation exists for egocentric look-back retrieval.

\paragraph{The architectural question.}
Three properties define the right retrieval substrate for a wearable
assistant: \emph{bounded memory}---per-cell state independent of
recording duration; \emph{spatial locality}---the index reflects the
user's physical trajectory, answering ``what places?'' as naturally as
``what moments?''; and \emph{streaming ingestion}---frames arrive
one-at-a-time, the index updates in-place, and queries are served
without re-indexing.

\paragraph{Probabilistic Spatial Memory.}
We propose \textbf{\psm{}}, a streaming spatial-temporal index for
egocentric look-back retrieval built on three data structures over an H3
hex-grid tessellation: (1)~\emph{reservoir-sampled exemplars} per cell
retain a fixed-size embedding sample; (2)~a \emph{time-decayed ring
buffer of HyperLogLog sketches} per cell tracks unique-event cardinality
over recent windows; (3)~a \emph{per-cell capacity bound}
(\caponek{}) limits how many top-$K$ slots one cell may fill---enforcing
strict spatial diversity at $\texttt{cap}{=}1$ and recovering
unconstrained top-$K$ at $\texttt{cap}{=}K$. Memory is bounded per cell,
ingestion is streaming, and top-$K$ search returns $K$ candidate
$(t,\mathrm{lat},\mathrm{lng})$ triples in sub-millisecond host-CPU
wall-time on both a synthetic stress test and the measured Nymeria
\psm{} state (\S\ref{sec:limitations} scopes the missing on-device
measurement).

\paragraph{The PSM--MLLM prefilter pipeline.}
Multimodal LLMs (Gemini~3, Claude~4, GPT-4o) achieve strong
single-frame grounding yet remain brittle on egocentric temporal
grounding over long
video~\cite{cheng2024videgothink,chen2025groundedmultihop}.
We compose \psm{} as a \emph{prefilter}: it returns top-$K$
spatial-temporal candidates from its bounded index; the MLLM reranks
these $K$ frames and emits the localized answer. The two contributions
are largely orthogonal---\psm{} supplies recall, the MLLM supplies
localization precision.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{\psm{} architecture:} a bounded-memory streaming
    spatial-temporal index combining H3 cells, time-decayed HLL ring
    buffers, and reservoir-sampled exemplars
    (\S\ref{sec:method}).

  \item \textbf{\caponek{} knob + oracle sanity check:}
    at the bounded $R{=}128$ operating point, \texttt{cap}=$K$
    reaches 21/187 hits vs.\ brute-force CLIP's 25/187; at oracle
    reservoir size $R{=}1024$, it recovers the same 25/187 hits with identical
    mIoU, confirming the expected top-$K$ equivalence when reservoir
    eviction is removed. $\texttt{cap}{=}1$ paired with
    Gemini rerank achieves mIoU\,0.069,
    statistically indistinguishable from brute-force 0.074
    (bootstrap $\Delta{=}{-}0.005$, 95\,\% CI
    $[-0.032,{+}0.021]$) (\S\ref{sec:results-main}).

  \item \textbf{Multi-corpus generalization + vanilla-MLLM baseline:}
    13/14 street-scale sessions across 3 sensor stacks and 3 encoders
    pass monotone acceptance
    (\S\ref{sec:results-multi-corpus},
    Table~\ref{tab:multi-corpus-h3}).
    \psm{}'s query-guided retrieval provides the spatial-temporal
    prefilter that uniform-sampling MLLMs lack: at matched $K{=}8$,
    \psm{} reaches every session that vanilla Gemini misses, including
    4 where uniform frames never land in a GT interval
    (\S\ref{sec:results-vanilla-mllm},
    Fig.~\ref{fig:f2-psm-vs-mllm}).
    Open-source under MIT (\S\ref{sec:conclusion} footnote).
\end{itemize}
