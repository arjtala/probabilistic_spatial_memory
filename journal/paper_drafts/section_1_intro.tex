\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A wearable AI assistant sees the world through the user's eyes,
continuously, for hours per day. To answer a look-back question
---``where did I leave my keys?'', ``what was that street sign?'',
``when did I last see Maria?''--- the assistant needs to find the
relevant moments from its own past. The straightforward solution is
to embed every frame the camera saw with a vision--language encoder
(CLIP, SigLIP, EVA) and run a top-$K$ cosine-similarity retrieval
over the resulting embedding bank. This works, but the embedding
bank grows linearly in recording duration: at a 1\,fps sampling and
CLIP-L's 768-dim float32 embeddings, an hour of egocentric video
occupies $\sim$10\,MB; a typical day, $\sim$200\,MB; the wearable's
on-device limit binds in days, not weeks. The substrate is not
appropriate for a bounded-memory streaming setting.

\paragraph{The architectural question.} What does the right
retrieval substrate look like for a wearable AI assistant? Three
properties matter. First, \emph{bounded memory}: per-cell state
that does not grow with recording duration. Second, \emph{spatial
locality}: the assistant should be able to answer
``what places did I visit?'' as cleanly as it answers ``what
moments matter?''; the index should reflect the user's actual
trajectory through physical space. Third, \emph{streaming
ingestion}: frames arrive one at a time, the index updates
in-place, and queries can be served at any time without
re-indexing.

\paragraph{Probabilistic Spatial Memory.}
We propose \textbf{PSM}, a streaming spatial-temporal index for
egocentric look-back retrieval. PSM combines three probabilistic
data structures over an H3 hex-grid tessellation of the wearer's
geographic trajectory:
(1) \emph{reservoir-sampled exemplars} per H3 cell preserve a
fixed-size sample of the CLIP embeddings the wearer captured in
that place;
(2) a \emph{time-decayed ring buffer of HyperLogLog sketches} per
cell tracks unique-event cardinality over recent time windows; and
(3) a \emph{per-cell capacity bound} (\texttt{per\_cell\_cap})
governs how many top-$K$ retrieval slots a single H3 cell may fill,
implementing strict spatial diversity at \texttt{cap}=1 and
permissive top-$K$-across-all-exemplars at \texttt{cap}=$K$.
The substrate is bounded in memory (fixed per cell, even as
recording duration grows unboundedly), streaming
(per-frame ingest, no global re-indexing), and trivially
queryable: a top-$K$ search returns $K$ candidate $(t, \mathrm{lat},
\mathrm{lng})$ triples in sub-millisecond C-side wall-time.

\paragraph{The PSM--MLLM prefilter pipeline.}
Modern multimodal LLMs (Gemini~3, Claude~4, GPT-4o) achieve strong
single-frame visual grounding but remain brittle on egocentric
video reasoning and temporal grounding over long-context
video~\cite{cheng2024videgothink,chen2025groundedmultihop}.
For wearable look-back, this is exactly the failure mode: the
question asks ``when?'' and the MLLM cannot answer reliably without
seeing every frame, which the wearable cannot afford to ship to a
remote API. We propose PSM as a \emph{prefilter} for the MLLM:
PSM returns its top-$K$ spatial-temporal candidates over the
bounded-memory index; the MLLM reranks the $K$ candidate frames
and emits the localized answer. The architecture composes
multiplicatively: PSM provides the candidate set (recall), the
MLLM provides localization precision within the set, and the two
contributions are largely orthogonal.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Probabilistic Spatial Memory:} a streaming
    bounded-memory spatial-temporal index for egocentric video,
    combining H3 cells, time-decayed ring-buffer HLL sketches, and
    reservoir-sampled exemplars (\S\ref{sec:method}).
  \item \textbf{The \texttt{per\_cell\_cap} knob:} a new
    operating-point parameter that smoothly trades strict spatial
    diversity for embedding-similarity precision between two
    endpoints (\texttt{cap}=1 vs \texttt{cap}=$K$), reducing to
    plain top-$K$-over-all-exemplars at \texttt{cap}=$K$.
    Empirically, \texttt{cap}=$K$ recovers brute-force CLIP
    retrieval accuracy exactly on a 187-question Nymeria
    benchmark; \texttt{cap}=1 trades 5.4 percentage points of
    Hit@5 for spatial diversity (\S\ref{sec:results-main}).
  \item \textbf{PSM\,$\rightarrow$\,Gemini at \texttt{cap}=1 matches
    brute-force CLIP on localization mIoU on the highest-mobility
    session.} On \texttt{shelby\_arroyo\_act0} the bounded-memory
    operating point paired with a single Gemini-3.1-Pro rerank
    achieves exemplar mIoU@5 of 0.073, statistically indistinguishable
    from brute-force CLIP's 0.074. On lower-mobility sessions the
    permissive \texttt{cap}=$K$ operating point retains a margin
    (Table~\ref{tab:multisession}); the MLLM rerank lifts
    exemplar mIoU 30--60\% relative across all four sessions
    (\S\ref{sec:results-main}).
  \item \textbf{Multi-session generalization:} the
    \texttt{per\_cell\_cap} effect replicates across 4 Nymeria
    sessions spanning the street-, building-, and room-scale
    mobility tiers (\S\ref{sec:results-multisession}).
  \item \textbf{Open-source release:} the C-side PSM engine
    (\texttt{src/core/spatial\_memory.c}, $\sim$700 LoC) and the
    Python extraction/eval pipeline are released under MIT
    (\S\ref{sec:reproducibility}).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Workshop scope alignment.}
This work targets three of the Wearables AI Workshop's listed
research areas. \emph{Long-Context \& Real-Time Interactions:}
PSM's reservoir + ring-buffer state is fixed-size per cell and
sub-millisecond per query, addressing the bounded-memory side of
the ``memory bottlenecks for sustained dialog'' theme.
\emph{Persistent Scene and Object Memory from Video:}
H3 + ring-buffer is a time-aware spatial representation of the
user's environment, designed for retrieval. \emph{Efficient AI
/ Edge Computing:} the engine's TurboQuant 2/3/4-bit exemplar
codec compresses per-cell exemplar storage 5--10$\times$ at no
Hit@5 cost, suitable for on-device deployment.
