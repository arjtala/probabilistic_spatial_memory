\begin{abstract}
A wearable AI assistant captures hours of egocentric video per day;
answering a look-back question (``where did I leave my keys?'')
requires retrieving the relevant moments under strict on-device
memory bounds. We propose \textbf{Probabilistic Spatial Memory}
(\psm), a streaming spatial-temporal index combining an H3 hex-grid
tessellation, a time-decayed ring buffer of HyperLogLog sketches per
cell, and reservoir-sampled CLIP-embedding exemplars. \psm{} exposes
a novel \caponek{} knob that smoothly trades strict spatial diversity
for embedding-similarity precision; the permissive endpoint
(\texttt{cap}=$K$) reduces to brute-force retrieval over all
in-reservoir exemplars. The
spatial-axis discrimination generalizes across \textbf{14 street-scale
sessions} drawn from \textbf{3 distinct sensor stacks} (SLOPER4D
head-mounted LiDAR, Nymeria + LookOut~\cite{pan2025lookout} Project
Aria MPS) and \textbf{3 encoder families} (laion CLIP-L / CLIP-bigG,
Google SigLIP~2~\cite{tschannen2025siglip2}): 13/14 sessions pass a
monotone-r10$\to$r12 Hit\,@\,5 acceptance with $\geq 4$\,pp lift on
at least one encoder. At matched candidate budget ($K{=}8$),
\psm{} \emph{beats} vanilla Gemini~3.1~Pro retrieval by
$1{-}\infty\times$ on every one of these 14 sessions (median
$\sim 4\times$, with 5 sessions where vanilla MLLM Hit\,@\,5 is
zero because the uniform $K{=}8$ frames structurally never land
in a $\sim$3\,s GT interval). The C engine ($\sim$700 LoC) and
Python eval harness are released under MIT.

\keywords{Egocentric video \and Spatial memory \and Bounded memory \and
Look-back QA \and Retrieval substrate \and Wearable AI.}
\end{abstract}
