\begin{abstract}
\noindent
% TODO: tighten abstract to ~200 words once §5 numbers stabilize
% (post-MLLM multi-session sweep, post-Aria Gen 2 annotation).
% Current draft is paper-spec sized but unedited prose.
A wearable AI assistant captures hours of egocentric video per day;
answering a look-back question (``where did I leave my keys?'')
requires retrieving the relevant moments from a large frame
stream under strict on-device memory bounds. Brute-force CLIP
retrieval over the full embedding bank grows linearly with
recording duration, which is structurally inappropriate for the
streaming bounded-memory wearable setting. We propose
\textbf{Probabilistic Spatial Memory} (\psm), a streaming spatial-
temporal index combining an H3 hex-grid tessellation, a time-
decayed ring buffer of HyperLogLog sketches per cell, and
reservoir-sampled CLIP-embedding exemplars. \psm{} exposes a
\caponek{} knob that smoothly trades strict spatial diversity
(\texttt{cap}=1: each top-$K$ slot a distinct H3 cell) for
permissive embedding-similarity precision
(\texttt{cap}=$K$: reduces to brute-force over all reservoir
exemplars). On a 187-question subset of the public Nymeria
egocentric benchmark, \psm{} at \texttt{cap}=$K$ recovers brute-
force CLIP retrieval exactly (13.4\% Hit@5, same 25 questions);
\psm{} at the bounded-memory default \texttt{cap}=1 paired with a
single Gemini-3.1-Pro rerank achieves localization mIoU
indistinguishable from brute-force CLIP's (0.073 vs 0.074), at
one-fifth the candidate budget. The operating-point effect
generalizes across four Nymeria sessions spanning the street-,
building-, and room-scale mobility tiers. The engine ($\sim$700
LoC of portable C) and Python eval harness are released under MIT.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Reproducibility}
\label{sec:reproducibility}
All experiments use the
\href{https://github.com/anonymized/probabilistic_spatial_memory}{released
PSM engine + Python harness}.
Key reproduction commands:
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{0pt}\small
  \item \texttt{python scripts/sweep\_per\_cell\_cap.py} ---
    operating-point sweep on \texttt{shelby\_arroyo\_act0}
    (Tables~\ref{tab:headline}, \ref{tab:ex-ablation}).
  \item \texttt{python scripts/multisession\_per\_cell\_cap\_sweep.py}
    --- four-session generalization (Table~\ref{tab:multisession}).
  \item \texttt{python scripts/eval\_psm\_mllm.py ... --mllm gemini}
    --- PSM\,$\rightarrow$\,Gemini rerank
    (Table~\ref{tab:headline}, last two rows).
\end{itemize}
